01 — Foundation

Mission Statement & Core Values

"As first responders we carry the most important whitelisted role in the city — we are the lifeline that keeps it going. Helping anyone, anywhere, by any means to the best of our abilities."
🤝
Teamwork
Working together in every situation — from personal interactions to emergency response on the field.
📡
Communication
Keeping transmissions clean and precise to maintain efficiency and eliminate confusion.
⚖️
Mutual Respect
Treating everyone with the same respect you expect to receive. No exceptions, no bias.
Our Goal
As the neutral force of the city, EMS treats everyone equally — law enforcement, civilians, and criminal organizations alike. Our duty is not to judge but to serve with compassion and without bias. Each individual on our team is as important as the whole.
Roleplay First
High RP or low RP — we stick to the job at hand and create a fun, enjoyable experience for all parties involved. RP is at the center of every interaction we have in the city.

06 — Standards

Duty Standards & Conduct

All EMS personnel are expected to maintain professionalism at all times — on duty and off. These standards apply from your first shift onward.

🎽 Uniform & Equipment
Red Medical BagBandagesDefibrillatorBracesIFAKSurgical KitIce PackPain-Killers
Always wear the official uniform from the lockers while on duty.
Stock up on all equipment before heading into the field.
Return to base to restock if supplies run low during a shift.
🚑 Priority Response Order
1EMS Members — always our top priority.
2SASP — drop everything and respond if they call over radio.
3All other dispatches in normal order.
🚗 Driving Rules
Drive as fast and safely as possible at all times.
Adhere to traffic rules When sirens are off.
Do not misuse sirens without a valid reason.
Replace damaged vehicles promptly. Return to garage when not in use.
Treat EMS vehicles with care — they represent the department.
📋 General Rules
During prolonged breaks, change into civilian clothes and go off duty.
KOS events: no EMS uniform, no EMS vehicles, no EMS services within the area.
Hired events: forward all offers to High Command first.
Firearms: permitted with a valid licence — self-defence only. Always clip footage.
Always respond to dispatches as quickly as possible regardless of distance.
07 — Integrity

Corruption & Prohibited Actions

EMS does not permit corruption of any kind. We favor roleplay over rule-play — clip incidents and report to HR. Distinguish between accidents and deliberate misconduct.

✕ Strictly Prohibited
Responding faster to gang contacts or personal associates
Allowing gang members to ride along in EMS vehicles
Providing free services based on affiliation
Participating in criminal activity on or off duty
Invoice farming or standing idle to accumulate pay
Distributing whitelisted EMS items to unauthorized individuals
Engaging in off-duty EMS activity
Any form of violence or harassment representing EMS
ℹ How to Handle Violations
If you witness corruption or rule-breaking, clip the footage immediately.
Report all incidents directly to HR — anonymity is always respected.
Play along in RP first; report after. Not every action is malicious.
When in doubt, contact HR — it is always better to report than to stay silent.
EMS is a safe space — no retaliation for good-faith reports.

09 — Leadership Pipeline

Department Management System (2IC)

Each Head of Department may appoint a Second-in-Command to strengthen leadership and ensure consistent day-to-day operations across the department.

2IC Responsibilities
Support the Department Head in leadership and decision-making.
Maintain consistency and efficiency within the department at all times.
Eligible for promotion to Captain rank over time.
Opportunity to join High Command with sustained strong performance.
Department Heads are encouraged to identify suitable candidates and move forward when ready.
Why This System Exists
📈
Expand High Command
Opens new leadership pathways for dedicated and active members.
🌍
Timezone Coverage
Better manage time zone differences to ensure 24/7 coverage.
⚙️
Operational Flexibility
Improve reliability and flexibility across all active departments.
🛡️
Stability
Ensure departments run smoothly even when the Head is unavailable.
10 — Absence Policy

Leave of Absence (LOA)

Proactively informing HR of pending inactivity is always preferred over being removed from the roster without notice. The door always remains open.

LOA may be granted for up to two weeks. For longer absences, contact HR directly with details.
Max absence without LOA: 3 days (below 4A) · 5 days (Supervisors) · 7 days (High Command).
Every request must include both a start date and an end date. No open-ended LOAs accepted.
Members removed for inactivity are welcome to reapply when they return. No permanent penalty.
Submit LOA requests directly to HR in the department Discord or contact them in-city.
